About

I'm a Microengineering graduate from EPFL with a focus on robotics, control, and applied machine learning. From predictive maintenance models for AXPO's industrial valve fleet to high-frequency trading analytics, I build systems where data and hardware converge to deliver measurable performance.

My portfolio spans autonomous mechatronic rigs, embedded wearables, and simulation-heavy software. Projects such as the DYNABAL dynamometer, self-regulating ski jacket, wireless sensor networks, and FPGA-based interfaces reflect hands-on experience moving from CAD and PCB design through fabrication, testing, and deployment.

I thrive in multidisciplinary teamsaligning robotics labs, finance analysts, and manufacturing workshops. Whether mentoring robotics projects, leading cleanroom builds, or turning deep-learning research into actionable dashboards, I translate complex constraints into clear plans that keep teams shipping resilient products.

Assignment

  • Multilingual text preprocessing, explainable AI overlays, model monitoring
  • Ethical AI considerations for moderation workflows

Deep RL Algorithm Benchmark

  • reinforcement learning
  • benchmarking
  • openai gym

Goal

Evaluate modern deep RL algorithms across CartPole-v1, Pendulum-v1, and MountainCarContinuous-v0, capturing learning speed, stability, and computational cost.

Method

Implemented agents in PyTorch with shared utilities for replay buffers, exploration schedules, and normalisation. Ran 30 seeds per algorithm and plotted confidence intervals to compare convergence.

Insights

  • SAC delivered fastest convergence on continuous control with low variance
  • PPO exhibited robust stability but higher sample consumption
  • Automated hyper-parameter sweeps and logging with Weights & Biases
